# Style Guide

## Voice

- Professional, direct, and efficient.
- Practical rather than theoretical.
- Confident without hype.
- Platform-native and easy to read on mobile.
- No filler, no long setup, no generic motivational language.

## Copywriting rules

Use clear, direct, high-conversion social media language.

Avoid:

- corporate jargon
- long paragraphs
- vague claims
- weak hooks
- over-explaining
- generic motivational language
- clickbait that does not match the content

Prefer:

- specificity
- contrast
- curiosity
- clear outcomes
- short sentences
- strong verbs
- reader-focused language
- save-worthy structure

## Image copy limits

- Cover headline: under 7 words.
- Body headlines: short, punchy, and concrete.
- Supporting text: 1 to 2 lines max.
- Each image: one idea, one focal point, one job.
- CTA image: one clear next step.

## Visual direction rules

Design for mobile-first viewing.

Use:

- strong hierarchy
- large readable headlines
- short supporting text
- clear focal point
- consistent color palette from brand context
- enough negative space
- strong contrast
- premium composition
- platform-appropriate layout

Avoid:

- tiny text
- overcrowded layouts
- too many icons
- generic stock-photo energy
- inconsistent typography
- low-contrast text
- multi-image previews
- grids
- collages
- contact sheets

## Strategic quality standards

Every post series must be:

- easy to understand
- easy to move through
- visually consistent
- mobile-readable
- strategically aligned with the user's goal
- specific to the target audience
- not generic
- not text-heavy
- not visually cluttered
- strong on the first image
- clear on the final CTA image

## Factuality

- Do not invent statistics, quotes, case studies, client names, endorsements, or citations.
- For current, statistical, legal, medical, financial, or high-stakes claims, use reliable sources when available and cite them.
- If a claim cannot be verified, soften it or remove it.
- When using source content, preserve the user's meaning and avoid adding unsupported claims.
